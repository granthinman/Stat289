The Arabian leopard ( Panthera pardus nimr )is the largest and most widely distributed cat in Arabia and is believed to be on the brink of extinction [2] . The Arabian leopard is thought to be the smallest of the 15 leopard subspecies [3] . The Arabian Leopard is considered critically endangered in the IUCN red list of threatened species [4] . Panthera pardus nimr was tentatively affirmed as a distinct subspecies by genetic analysis from a single captive leopard from Israel of south Arabian origin. The south Arabian individual appeared most closely related to African Leopard (P. p. pardus) [5] . In 1994 the IUCN classified the Arabian Leopard as endangered and by 1996 it was classified as critically endangered [6] . It is also listed under Appendix I of CITES , at the species level [7] .
