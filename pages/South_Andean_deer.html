South Andean deer

The south Andean deer ( Hippocamelus bisulcus ), also known as the southern guemal , [2] Chilean huemul or güemul ( / ˈ w eɪ m uː l / WAY -mool , Spanish:  [weˈmul] ), is an endangered species of deer native to the mountains of Argentina and Chile . One of two mid-sized deer of the Hippocamelus genus , the south Andean deer ranges across the high mountainsides and cold valleys of the Andes . The distribution and habitat , behaviour, and diet of the deer have all been the subject of study. The viability of the small remaining population is an outstanding concern to researchers.
