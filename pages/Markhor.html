The markhor ( Capra falconeri ; Persian / Urdu : مارخور; Pashto : مرغومی ‎ marǧūmay ) is a large species of wild goat that is found in northeastern Afghanistan , northern Pakistan ( Gilgit-Baltistan and northern Khyber Pakhtunkhwa ), some parts of Indian Jammu and Kashmir , southern Tajikistan and southern Uzbekistan . The species is classed by the IUCN as Endangered , as there are fewer than 2,500 mature individuals and the numbers have continued to decline by an estimated 20% over two generations. [1] The markhor is the national animal of Pakistan.
